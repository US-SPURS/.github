# Maintainers

This document lists the maintainers of US-SPURS open source projects.

## What is a Maintainer?

Maintainers are responsible for the ongoing maintenance and development of US-SPURS open source projects. They have write access to repositories and are responsible for reviewing pull requests, managing issues, and ensuring the health and quality of the codebase.

## Responsibilities

Maintainers are expected to:

* Review and merge pull requests in a timely manner
* Respond to issues and provide guidance to contributors
* Maintain code quality and enforce coding standards
* Ensure security best practices are followed
* Help define project roadmap and priorities
* Foster a welcoming and inclusive community
* Uphold the [Code of Conduct](CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md)
* Participate in discussions about project direction

## Current Maintainers

### Project Maintainers

Project-specific maintainers are listed in the MAINTAINERS.md file of each individual repository.

## Becoming a Maintainer

Maintainers are typically promoted from active contributors who have demonstrated:

* Consistent, high-quality contributions
* Deep understanding of the project
* Commitment to the project's success
* Alignment with project values and goals
* Strong communication skills
* Collaborative approach

If you're interested in becoming a maintainer, please:

1. Continue making regular contributions
2. Help review pull requests from others
3. Assist with issue triage and support
4. Engage constructively with the community
5. Reach out to current maintainers to express your interest

## Stepping Down

Maintainers who need to step down from their responsibilities should:

1. Notify other maintainers and the Open Source Program Office
2. Help transition their responsibilities
3. Update this document to reflect the change

Emeritus maintainers who have stepped down are recognized for their contributions and may be consulted on major decisions.

## Contact

For questions about maintainership or to discuss becoming a maintainer, please contact opensource@us-spurs.gov.
